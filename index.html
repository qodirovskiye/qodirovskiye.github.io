About

01 / 05

I grew up in Dushanbe, Tajikistan, and spent most of my teenage years doing competitive mathematics — representing my country at international olympiads and later teaching the next cohort of students the same craft.

Today I study at the Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen, School of Data Science, on a full Honourable Scholarship — and I was the first Tajik student admitted. The combination of olympiad-trained problem solving and applied data work is what I bring to everything I do.

At Mortar, I'm a co-founder working on the research and data side — helping capture what matters from industrial environments, validating it in the field, and turning those learning loops into shipped capability alongside my co-founders.

Outside studies and Mortar, I try to stay active on both sides of life — running structured outreach for prospective CUHK-Shenzhen applicants, and playing football for the college team.

Now

  • Learning Mandarin and Italian — the sixth and seventh, after Tajik, Russian, English, Farsi, and Arabic.
